# Nestly advertising plan: most subscribers for the least money

*Drafted 19 Jul 2026. Targets Australia first, then other English-speaking countries.*

## Where Nestly stands

Nestly is free during early access, runs as a web app (no app store), and has a built-in multiplier: one signup is a whole household, so every converted "customer" typically brings 3 to 5 users with them. Trip guests and invited relatives see the product from the inside before they ever hear an ad. That makes Nestly unusually well suited to word-of-mouth style growth, and it means paid ads should be the second lever, not the first.

Being a web app is also an advantage for advertising: there is no app store tax, no install friction (the invite link opens instantly), and campaigns point at a normal web page, which is cheaper to run and easier to measure than app-install campaigns.

## Phase 1: free channels first (start now)

**Short-form video is the main game.** TikTok, Instagram Reels and YouTube Shorts are where AU parents aged 25 to 45 actually are, and organic reach there is still real. The videos you already plan for each tour section are the content: 20 to 40 second screen recordings with a hook. The founder story is the differentiator, so say it out loud: "I'm a dad in Sydney and I built one app for my family because we had six group chats and a spreadsheet." Post 2 to 3 times a week. Feature demos that do well organically become your ad creative later, already proven.

Hook ideas that map to existing features: "The fridge note that writes your shopping list", "We planned a week of dinners in 90 seconds", "Two families, one villa, zero awkward money chats", "My bank sorts itself now, I just tick".

**Communities, carefully.** Australian parenting and budgeting groups on Facebook, plus Reddit (r/AusFinance, r/AusParents, r/mealprep). Do not drop links cold. Answer real questions (meal planning, splitting holiday costs, family budgeting) and mention Nestly where it genuinely fits. One good comment in a big AU budgeting thread outperforms $50 of ads.

**Search presence.** People actively search for this category. Create simple pages targeting "family organiser app Australia", "shared shopping list app", "meal planner app family", and comparison pages against the incumbents (Cozi, FamilyWall, OurHome). The tour page already does half this job; comparison pages do the rest. This is slow to start and then free forever.

**Launch moments.** A Product Hunt launch, listings on AlternativeTo and similar directories, and a short pitch to AU outlets that cover local founders (Startup Daily, SmartCompany, Kidspot and mum-focused blogs). "Sydney butcher builds family app" is a story journalists can use.

**Sharpen the built-in loop.** Invites already exist; add a share prompt at the moment of delight (first "All sorted!" screen, first generated shopping list) and a "start your own nest" banner for trip guests. This costs nothing and compounds with everything above.

## Phase 2: paid ads (switch on once videos exist)

**Meta (Facebook + Instagram) first.** It has the best parent targeting in Australia. Current AU benchmarks: average CPC around $1.47 AUD, average CPM around $9.80, and Reels placements are the bargain at roughly $6.20 to $6.30 CPM, about 45% cheaper than Instagram Feed. Start with one conversion campaign (signup event), Advantage+ placements weighted to Reels, audience "parents 28 to 45, Australia", $10 to $20 per day. Use your proven organic videos as the creative. A realistic early target is a signup (one household) for under $5 to $8; kill any ad set that runs double that for a week.

**TikTok second.** Slightly cheaper reach and it rewards the same creative. Use Spark Ads to boost the organic posts that already performed, rather than making separate ad creative.

**Google Search as a small, permanent trickle.** $3 to $5 a day on exact-match, high-intent terms only: "family organiser app", "shared shopping list app", "cozi alternative". Low volume but these clicks are people already looking for exactly this.

**Skip for now:** display networks, X, LinkedIn, podcast sponsorships, YouTube pre-roll, influencer retainers. All of them burn budget at this stage. One exception worth testing later: micro mum-influencers in AU (5k to 50k followers) paid in free lifetime accounts rather than cash.

## Geography

Stay Australia-only until the funnel works (ads to signups to active households at a known cost). Then expand in this order: New Zealand (near-identical audience, cheap), United Kingdom (CPCs about 18% below Australia), Canada, and the United States last. The US is the biggest prize but also the most expensive and competitive (AU CPCs are about 23% cheaper than US), so go there once the numbers are proven. Localise nothing but the currency examples at first.

## Measurement (do this before spending a dollar)

Install the Meta Pixel plus Conversions API and fire a signup event on household creation. Tag every link (UTMs) including the ones in your videos' bios. Track one number above all: cost per activated household, where activated means they invited at least one member or connected one account in week 1. Your analytics tracking (the Track component) already logs landing and login paths, so extend it to record the UTM source on signup.

## When money enters the picture

Benchmarks from RevenueCat's 2026 report (115,000+ apps) worth designing around when you start charging: apps with a hard paywall convert about 10.7% of installs to paid versus 2.1% for freemium, but long free trials of 17 to 32 days convert best of all (around 42.5% of trials). For a family app, the natural shape is a generous free trial of the full product for the whole household, then one household price, billed annually by default. Note also that first-year churn across the industry has worsened sharply, and the stickiest features are the ones with accumulated data: the bank feeds, the photo albums, the recipe book. Everything that fills Nestly with a family's own data is a retention feature, so the ads should push toward those moments, not just the signup.

## Budget scenarios (AUD, monthly)

**$0:** organic video plus communities plus the referral loop. Expect slow but real compounding; this is also the required warm-up for paid, because you need proven creative.

**$500 starter:** roughly $350 Meta conversions, $100 Google exact match, $50 boosting the best organic post. At a $5 to $8 cost per household that is 60 to 100 new households a month, meaning roughly 250 to 400 family members using Nestly.

**$1,500 growth:** scale the winning Meta ad sets, add TikTok Spark Ads, extend to NZ. Only step up to this once the starter budget holds its cost per household for two consecutive months.

## First 30 days

Week 1: set up Pixel plus signup event tracking, claim TikTok and Instagram handles, record the first two tour videos. Week 2: publish videos, join 5 AU parenting and budgeting groups, submit directory listings. Week 3: first Meta campaign at $10 a day using the best video. Week 4: review cost per household, double down on whichever video pulled, draft the Product Hunt launch.
